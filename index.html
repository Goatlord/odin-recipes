Yes Chef: A Collection of Recipes That Will Make You a Better Cook (Maybe)

Today we have for you, recipes from all parts of the world. Ranging from simple weeknight meals to elaborate dishes for special occasions. The flavors are timeless and the techniques are accessible to cooks of all levels. But don't worry if you're a beginner – we've got you covered with step-by-step instructions and helpful tips. Like here's a good tip: Never cook without a good hat. What if you're searing a steak and your head starts to get cold? Big mistake! Once you walk away that steak could be overcooked. Or undercooked. Right to jail. Right away. All because you didn't wear a good hat. You might as well cancel christmas.

Full disclosure: All of these recipes are pet friendly. Which is to say, your pet will want some of what you're making too! All recipes include deliberately non-specific amounts of ingredients because who has time for measuring? Just add stuff and see how it tastes. In the time it takes to measure out 3 tsp of salt, I could have ran to Sears and bought a whole set of reasonably priced vaccuum cleaner accessories. Did you know Sears is still around?

We hope you enjoy these recipes and that they inspire you to get in the kitchen and start cooking. Because we all know, cooking is the best way to ignore other problems that really should be taken care of. But who cares. You don't have time to stress about calling the DMV to ask for information that they aren't even sure of so you have to call again tomorrow after doing some research, but then once you find out what you need, it takes 3 weeks for them to actually file the papers so you can finally go there and pay $37 to reinstate your license. Why does it have to be so complicated, Leslie?
